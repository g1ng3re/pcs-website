Home Insurance

Buildings, contents, and the difference between them.

Buildings insurance covers the structure of your property: walls, roof, fitted kitchens and bathrooms, and permanent fixtures. Contents insurance covers everything you would take with you if you moved out. Most policies bundle the two, but you can buy them separately if it suits.

If you are buying a property with a mortgage, buildings insurance is a lender requirement. Contents is optional, but most people need it more than they expect.

Choosing Cover

What to look for in a policy.

Rebuild Value

Buildings cover should match the cost to rebuild, not the market value of the property. Most surveys will state this.

Sum Insured for Contents

Work out the cost to replace your possessions as new. Most people underestimate this significantly.

Accidental Damage

Often optional. Useful if you have children, expensive flooring, or large screens.

High Value Items

Items above a single article limit need to be specified individually. Jewellery, watches, bikes, and tech are the usual culprits.

Away From Home Cover

Useful if you take valuables out of the house regularly.

Legal and Home Emergency

Often available as low-cost add-ons. We tell you whether they are worth it for your case.

Landlords

If the property is let.

Standard home insurance does not cover rented property. Landlords need specialist cover that addresses property damage, loss of rent, public liability, and where applicable rent guarantee.

Common Questions

Buildings and Contents FAQs.

Do I need buildings insurance?

If you have a mortgage, yes. It is a lender requirement. It must be in place from the date you exchange contracts on a purchase.

Do I need contents insurance?

It is optional, but most people benefit from it. The cost to replace your possessions is usually higher than you expect.

How much should I insure my contents for?

Add up the cost to replace everything as new, not the second-hand value. Most people underinsure.

What is accidental damage cover?

Cover for one-off accidents such as spilling paint on a carpet or breaking a TV. Often optional and worth considering for households with children.

Do I need cover when renting out my property?

Yes. You need specialist landlord buildings cover. Standard home insurance does not apply to let property.

What is excess?

The amount you pay towards a claim. Higher excess usually means lower premiums. We help you set it sensibly.

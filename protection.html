Common Questions

Protection FAQs.

Do I really need life insurance?

If anyone depends on your income, a partner, children, or ageing parents, then yes. Life insurance ensures they are financially protected if you are no longer there.

What is the difference between life insurance and critical illness cover?

Life insurance pays out on death. Critical illness cover pays a lump sum if you are diagnosed with a serious condition such as cancer, heart attack, or stroke, while you are still alive.

How much cover do I need?

A general rule is around 10 times annual income for life cover, and up to 70% of monthly income for income protection. We calculate the right amount for your specific situation.

Can I get cover with a pre-existing condition?

Yes in most cases. Some conditions may be excluded or attract a higher premium, but we work with specialist insurers who consider a wide range of medical histories.

How much does protection cost?

Life cover can start from as little as £5 per month at younger ages and healthy profiles. We compare the whole market to find the right rate for your circumstances.

What happens if I miss a payment?

Most policies have a grace period. We advise clients to set up a direct debit and to contact us immediately if they are struggling, so we can explore options before the policy lapses.

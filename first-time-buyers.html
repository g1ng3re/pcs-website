First-Time Buyer Mortgages

What a first-time buyer mortgage actually is.

A first-time buyer mortgage is designed for people purchasing their first property. Lenders often offer more competitive rates, lower deposit options, and access to government schemes that are not available to existing homeowners.

As a whole of market broker, we compare every viable lender and recommend the deal that fits your income, deposit, and the property you want to buy. Not the deal that is easiest for us to process.

Available Schemes

Government schemes we advise on.

Shared Ownership

Buy a share of a property between 25% and 75% and pay rent on the remaining share. Useful when a full deposit on the open market is not realistic.

Right to Buy

If you are a council tenant you may be entitled to buy your home at a significant discount. We arrange Right to Buy mortgages regularly.

First Homes Scheme

First-time buyers can purchase a new build home at a discount of at least 30% compared to market value, where the scheme is available.

Right to Acquire

If you are a housing association tenant you may be able to purchase your home at a discount. Similar to Right to Buy, applied to housing association properties.

Eligibility criteria apply and schemes are subject to change. We will confirm which options are available to you during your consultation.

Common Questions

First-Time Buyer FAQs.

How much deposit do I need as a first-time buyer?

Most lenders accept a minimum of 5%. The larger your deposit, the better the rate you will typically receive. We will advise on the right level for your situation.

Can I get a mortgage on my own?

Yes. Single applicant mortgages are common. Borrowing will be based on your income alone, but plenty of lenders offer competitive rates for sole applicants.

How long does the process take?

First consultation to completion is typically 3 to 6 months, depending on the property and the speed of your solicitor. We keep things moving.

What is an Agreement in Principle?

An AIP is a statement from a lender confirming they would lend you a certain amount in principle. It signals to sellers and agents that you are a serious buyer.

Can I use a government scheme and a broker?

Yes. We advise on every major scheme and combine that with whole of market mortgage research to land the right outcome.

What costs should I budget for besides the deposit?

Solicitor fees, survey costs, stamp duty where applicable, broker fee, and moving costs. We will give you a full breakdown during your consultation.
